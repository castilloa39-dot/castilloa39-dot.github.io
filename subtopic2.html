Personalized Nutrition by Prediction of Glycemic Responses
Site Author
 David Zeevi and colleagues

Abstract
 The study focuses on how people respond differently to the same foods by analyzing blood glucose levels with continuous glucose monitoring (CGM). Researchers discovered that people have unique glycemic responses to identical meals, challenging the idea of ​​general dietary recommendations. The study highlights the importance of personalized nutrition and demonstrates how wearable devices provide real-time data to support and improve dietary choices. Through CGM technology, users have the opportunity to understand how their bodies react to specific foods and adjust their eating habits accordingly. The study underscores the potential of wearable technology to transform nutrition, suggesting that dietary recommendations should become more individualized.

 Future Directions for Integrative Objective Assessment of Eating Using Wearable Sensing Technology
Site Author
 Andy Skinner, Zoi Toumpakari, Christopher Stone, and Laura Johnson
Abstract
 The article examines how wearable sensor technology is used to improve the objective monitoring of people's eating habits. The authors explain that traditional dietary assessment methods are mostly based on self-reporting, which carries the risk of inaccuracies due to memory lapses or underestimation of intake. Wearable devices offer a more accurate alternative because they collect data continuously throughout the day. These devices also record physical actions related to eating. This type of technology helps researchers observe eating habits in real-life situations, rather than relying solely on memory. The article also analyzes how combining different types of wearable sensors can improve the accuracy of user nutrition data and provide a more comprehensive understanding of their eating habits. The article revealed that wearable technology can help improve nutrition research and allow for more accurate deficiency monitoring.
